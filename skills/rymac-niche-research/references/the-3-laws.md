# The 3 Laws of Landed Jobs — the default mechanism

This is RyMac's mechanism, published openly. Use it as-is, or replace it
with your own by writing `references/my-mechanism.md` in the same shape.

**What a mechanism is for.** A mechanism is the standard you believe
operators should hit. The gap between the standard and what a niche actually
does is your offer. Without one, niche research produces a pile of
complaints; with one, it produces a quantified gap you can price.

**Why these three.** Any business that takes inbound leads loses money in
exactly three places: how fast they respond, how long they keep trying, and
whether the message fits where the buyer is. Fix all three and the lead flow
does not have to change for revenue to go up. That last point is the whole
pitch: you are not selling more leads, you are selling the ones they already
paid for.

## Law 1 — Speed

**Headline:** The 5 Minutes That Change Everything
**Standard:** reach a new lead in under 5 minutes.

| Stat | Number |
|---|---|
| More likely to CONNECT (5 min vs 30 min) | **100x** |
| More likely to QUALIFY (5 min vs 30 min) | **21x** |
| Buy from whoever responds first | **78%** |
| Odds drop if you wait 5 to 10 minutes | **~400%** |
| Past 30 minutes | effectively gone |
| What the average business actually takes | **over 40 hours** |

**The decay curve**, relative likelihood of reaching and qualifying by
response time:

| 1 min | 5 min | 30 min | 1 hr | 24 hr | 40 hr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 100x | 21x | 1x | cold | cold | dead |

**Source, cite it exactly:** MIT (Dr. James Oldroyd) / InsideSales,
published by Harvard Business Review, study of 15,000+ leads. Corroborated
by the HBR 2,241-company study and various speed-to-lead datasets.

**The plain-English version:** the first 5 minutes are worth about 400% to
your bottom line. Wait 30 minutes and you are playing rock-paper-scissors
for the job. Take an hour and you just paid to leave a voicemail.

## Law 2 — Persistence

**Headline:** 80% of Jobs Land on Follow-Up #6
**Standard:** the money is in touches **5 to 12**.

| Stat | Number |
|---|---|
| Of deals need 5 to 12 touches to close | **~80%** |
| Salespeople who quit before the 5th follow-up | **92%** |
| Who quit after just ONE | **44%** |
| Who follow up more than 5 times | **only 8%** |
| Inquirers who will not buy for at least 3 months | **63%** |

**Scoring a niche against Law 2:**

| Touches today | Verdict |
|---|---|
| 1 | bottom 44%, badly broken |
| 2 to 4 | the 92% who quit before the money starts |
| 5 to 12 | the money zone, top 8% |
| 12 to 24 | over-delivering on purpose |

Note that 5 to 12 is the INDUSTRY math, where deals actually close. A
product that delivers 12 to 24 touches is deliberately going past the
standard. Never present the over-delivery number as the benchmark.

**Source:** InsideSales.com / Invesp follow-up data, widely republished
(HubSpot sales statistics and similar follow-up compilations).

## Law 3 — The Right Message

**Headline:** Your Customer's Journey Matters
**Standard:** the message changes with every touch AND runs across more than
one channel, matched to where the buyer sits on the awareness ladder.

| Stat | Number |
|---|---|
| Ready to buy RIGHT NOW at any moment | **only 3 to 5%** |
| Everyone else, burned by "ready to schedule?" | **95%** |

The ladder (Eugene Schwartz's 5 awareness stages), with what each rung
actually wants:

| Stage | What they want |
|---|---|
| Most Aware | product and price |
| Product Aware | discounts and deals |
| Solution Aware | claims and proof |
| Problem Aware | benefits and anxieties |
| Unaware | stories and secrets |

**The framing:** stop talking to 100% of your pipeline like they are Most
Aware when only 3 to 5% are. Good follow-up is a sequence: education for the
problem-aware, proof for the product-aware, a clean offer for the most-
aware. Build it once, let it run forever.

This is the law almost nobody in any trade satisfies. When research confirms
a niche runs one channel with one repeated message, that is the widest gap
on the board and usually the wedge.

## The loss model

Any profit-gained figure this skill produces should use one model, so the
research, the sales call, and any on-page calculator all say the same number.

```
winnable   = monthly leads x 0.60
lost jobs  = winnable x LOSS RATE (from the table below)
lost money = lost jobs x average job value
```

| How fast they answer a new lead | Loss rate |
|---|---|
| Under 5 minutes | **0.04** |
| Within 30 minutes | **0.28** |
| Within an hour | **0.42** |
| Same day / next day | **0.60** |

**Recovery math for an offer:**

```
recovered jobs    = winnable x (their current loss rate - 0.04)
recovered revenue = recovered jobs x average job value
profit gained     = recovered revenue x GROSS margin
break-even jobs   = offer price / (average job value x gross margin)
```

Gross margin, not net, on the recovery side: a job the shop was already
going to lose adds no new overhead, so recovered dollars drop at
contribution margin. Net margin is only for judging whether they can afford
the offer at all.

**Label discipline, and please keep this honest.** The 100x, 21x, 78%, 92%,
44%, 8%, 3 to 5%, and 63% figures ARE the studies and can be attributed. The
0.60 winnable factor and the loss-rate table are **one operator's
calibration**, shaped by the studies but not published by them. They are a
sane starting point, not gospel. Adjust them to your own close data as you
get it, and never attribute them to Harvard or MIT.

## Mapping a complaint to a law

The niche never names the law. They describe a symptom. The translation is
the job:

| What they say | Law broken | How far past the line |
|---|---|---|
| "Leads suck, they never answer when I call the next day" | Law 1 | next day is past 40 hours, the far end of the curve |
| "I called twice and gave up, they're tire kickers" | Law 2 | 2 touches, the 92% who quit before the money |
| "I follow up and still don't book" | Law 3 | right persistence, wrong message, treating everyone as Most Aware |
| "I'm too busy on jobs to answer the phone" | Law 1 | structural, every after-hours call is a full loss |
| "The lead was shared with 4 other guys" | Law 1 | 78% went to whoever called first, and it was not them |

Do this for every major complaint theme surfaced by the pain-map agent. A
complaint with no law attached is unfinished work.

## Writing your own mechanism

Copy this file to `references/my-mechanism.md` and replace the contents.
Whatever your framework is, it needs four things to work in this skill:

1. **Standards.** 2 to 4 of them, each stated as a number an operator either
   hits or misses. "Be responsive" is not a standard. "Under 5 minutes" is.
2. **Sourced stats.** Real citations for why each standard matters. This is
   what lets the research quote a figure a prospect can go verify.
3. **Scoring tiers.** What counts as broken, partial, and meeting the
   standard, so a researched average can be graded.
4. **A loss model.** The arithmetic that turns a gap into dollars, with the
   parts that are yours clearly separated from the parts that are sourced.
