## Pain-led / enemy-status-quo

*The "stop paying a penalty for growth" register — Allodra's native category.*

| Headline | Company | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Email sucked for years. Not anymore — we fixed it." | HEY (classic, 2020) | Enemy = the whole category. Agitate then relieve in two beats. The most-cited enemy headline in SaaS. |
| "We finally fixed your email + calendar!" (sub: "Gmail, Outlook, and Apple got complacent…") | HEY (live) | Names the incumbents as villains; "finally" implies years of shared suffering. |
| "Your business is growing, and you're swamped. The way you're working isn't working anymore." | Basecamp (classic, 2017) | Pure PAS above the fold; mirrors the customer's inner monologue before mentioning the product. |
| "It's like milk, but made for humans." | Oatly | Villain-by-implication — the insult to the incumbent is embedded in the comparison. Got sued; the lawsuit became the marketing. |
| "Work communication that won't distract you all day" | Twist | Unnamed enemy is Slack. "[Category] that won't [known pain of the leader]." |
| "Share a video, not a calendar invite" | Loom (classic) | "X, not Y" — the villain (meetings) is something the reader already resents. |
| "No trackers, no javascript, no stylesheets. Just your words." | Bear Blog (sub) | Defined entirely by refusal. "No X, no Y, no Z. Just [what matters]." |
| "A Google Analytics alternative that's simple & privacy-first" | Fathom | Names the giant. "A [leader] alternative that's [the two things the leader isn't]." |
| "Make dental billing our job, not yours" | DayDream | Takes a hated task off the reader's plate in one line; niche word makes the right reader self-select. |
| "Maintaining code should be easier than writing it" | Dosu | "Should be" voices an injustice the reader already feels — indignation as hook. |
| "The collaboration layer your AI tools are missing." | Miro (live) | Creates a gap in the reader's stack rather than attacking a brand. |
| "Build an online business that still feels human" | Podia (live) | "Still" implies the enemy (soulless automation) without naming anyone. |
| "Create business software you actually want" | Glide | "Actually" is the knife — implies all current business software is unwanted. |
| "Issue tracking is dead" | Linear (campaign) | Declares the old category dead — category-villain move against Jira, implicitly. |

## Dream-outcome

| Headline | Company | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Be less busy" | Slack (classic v1) | Three words; sells the destination, not the chat app. |
| "Time is money. Save both." | Ramp | Hijacks a cliché, twists it in two words. Five words = the whole value prop. |
| "Easy scheduling ahead" | Calendly | After-state as a road sign — friction ends here. |
| "Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue." (sub: "…from your first transaction to your billionth.") | Stripe (live) | Category word + outcome; the subhead flatters ambition with specificity. |
| "Go from 0 to $1" | Gumroad (live) | Deliberately tiny promise — lowers the belief threshold instead of inflating the claim. Anti-hype specificity. |
| "Make your website a growth engine" | Webflow (live) | Reframes the artifact as a machine that produces revenue. |
| "The AI workspace that works for you." (sub: "Meet the night shift.") | Notion (live) | Double meaning + a metaphor that makes AI agents concrete. |
| "Superpowers, everywhere you work" | Superhuman (live) | Identity promise consistent with the name — but weaker than their classic (below); useful contrast. |

## Before → after rewrites

From Marketing Examples' "Rewriting landing pages with a pro copywriter" (Harry Dry + Annie Maguire) and documented homepage evolutions:

1. **Everydae (SAT prep):** "The next generation of SAT prep" → **"Acing the SAT"**. Kids don't care about your product positioning; sell the outcome in their words.
2. **Silva (headlamps):** product name as headline ("Trail Runner Free") → rewritten around night-trail adventures. "People don't get excited by a new headlamp. They get excited by new adventures."
3. **Banquist (chef-led classes):** vague title "that could mean fifty different things" → the unique mechanism made explicit. Vagueness wastes uniqueness.
4. **Ladybird Driving School:** differentiator (female instructors) buried mid-page → led with it. "Write the title only you can."
5. **Slack trajectory:** "Be less busy" (challenger, outcome) → "Where work happens" (incumbent, category) — benefit-first wins at launch; category-first only after dominance.
6. **Basecamp trajectory (55 homepages, 13 years):** 2004 "An elegant web-based project management and client extranet tool for creative services firms" (feature soup) → 2017 "Your business is growing, and you're swamped…" (pain-led PAS). The canonical weak→strong arc.
7. **Allodra (house example):** "Stop renting your CRM. Own it outright." (mechanism-led) → target register: **"Stop paying a penalty for growth."** (pain-led; the growth tax is the villain, the outcome is implied in the same breath).

## Stealable structures

- "X, not Y" — *Loom*
- "[Category] that won't [known pain of the leader]" — *Twist*
- "We finally fixed [category]" — *HEY*
- "[Cliché], [twist]" — *Ramp*
- "No X, no Y, no Z. Just [what matters]" — *Bear Blog*
- "[Task] should be easier than [other task]" — *Dosu*
- "Make [hated task] our job, not yours" — *DayDream*
- "A [leader] alternative that's [what the leader isn't]" — *Fathom*
- "Stop [status quo the reader resents]" — the house register
- "It's like [known thing], but [twist that indicts the incumbent]" — *Oatly*
- "[Thing you already want], [on autopilot / benefit]" — *Slite*
- "Go from [tiny honest number] to [tiny honest number]" — *Gumroad*

## Meta-observations

- The strongest enemy headlines **never punch down at the customer** — the villain is a tool, category, or incumbent, never the reader's own failure.
- **Enemy positioning is a challenger's weapon; category language is an incumbent's.** Every pain-led classic above comes from a brand's challenger phase. Allodra is a challenger — use the weapon.
- Nearly every big SaaS homepage in 2026 has drifted to AI-agent category framing — which means a sharp pain-led headline now stands out MORE, not less.
