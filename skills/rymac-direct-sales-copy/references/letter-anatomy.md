# The RyMac Letter Anatomy — 23 Beats

The beat-by-beat structure of the RyMac direct sales letter, extracted from the canonical specimen: the Sierra-6 letter to veteran home-service owners (`sierra6-site/index.html`, the `#letter` section — live at sierra6ai.com). Every letter written with this skill follows these beats in this order. The clothes change per offer; the bones never do.

## The beat map

The letter is one P.A.S. arc stretched over 23 beats. P = beats 1–7, A = beats 6–9 (agitation overlaps the tail of Problem), S = beats 10–23.

| # | Beat | Job | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Headline** | Identity + pain + enemy in one line | 1 line |
| 2 | **Salutation** | Identity bond ("Dear Fellow ___,") | 1 line |
| 3 | **"Sounds like you" bullets** | Self-identification: 4–6 pain QUESTIONS, each ending in `?` | 4–6 bullets |
| 4 | **Normalization** | "If so, you're not alone." + bold "Thousands of ___ struggle with the same issues every day." | 2 short ¶ |
| 5 | **Dream-outcome empathy run** | Anaphora "You want…" ×4–5, from business wants down to human wants (time, family, hobbies). End on the underlined identity line: "you're good at what you do." | 4–5 ¶ |
| 6 | **Agitation scenes** | 3–4 cinematic micro-scenes of the pain happening, each its own ¶. Include one enemy pull-quote (spoken aloud) and one italicized identity wound. | 5–8 ¶ |
| 7 | **Loop close** | The grind compressed: "Wake up. Rinse. Repeat." + tag question | 1 ¶ |
| 8 | **Pain agreement (THE PIVOT)** | Bold, short, agreement not solution: "It's aggravating as fire. I get it." | 1 ¶ |
| 9 | **Disqualification** | What I am NOT (the enemy's category): "I'm not an agency, and I don't sell leads." | 1 ¶ |
| 10 | **Who I am** | Name + nickname + one humanizing texture detail, cut off with "But…" | 1–2 ¶ |
| 11 | **Origin story** | First client: name, trade, what they risked, timeframe, dollar result → their quote as a pull-quote (ALL CAPS, brand color) → the aftermath | 3–4 ¶ |
| 12 | **Mission decision** | "I decided this is what I wanted to do. I wanted to help people." + "And since that day in [year]…" | 1–2 ¶ |
| 13 | **Scale claim + credibility** | Bold aggregate claim + borrowed authority humbly framed ("tracking me down… I had no idea I'd done anything 'special'") | 2–3 ¶ |
| 14 | **Niche-down repositioning** | "Now it's [year], and my focus is on [the reader's identity]. [Identity] like you." | 1 ¶ |
| 15 | **System reveal** | Mechanism NAME in bold ("The 3 Laws to Landed Jobs") + "My [system] will:" | 1–2 ¶ |
| 16 | **System bullets** | 5–6 OUTCOME bullets (never features), each killing a pain from beats 3/6 | 5–6 bullets |
| 17 | **Bonus** | The sweetener + quality vow ("I'll build it FREE… no 'half-stepping' here") | 1–2 ¶ |
| 18 | **Speed promise** | Bold, headline-formula restated: dream outcome + timeframe ("results the day it's deployed — as fast as 48 hours") | 1 ¶ |
| 19 | **Future-pacing** | "Imagine…" ×3, alternating bold/underline, each negating an earlier agitation scene one-for-one | 3 ¶ |
| 20 | **Price anchor + gift frame** | "Let me give you [time/sanity/dignity] back, for less than [everyday purchase]." | 1 ¶ |
| 21 | **Soft CTA** | Qualifier frame: "Let's have a 15-minute chat and see if it makes sense." + button | 1 ¶ + button |
| 22 | **Signoff + risk killers** | Locked signature + "✓ ___ · ✓ No Contracts · ✓ No Set-Up Fees" strip | 2 lines |
| 23 | **P.S.** | One metaphor that re-sells the CTA sideways ("Let me cook, and you eat.") + the link again | 1 ¶ |

## The annotated specimen

The Sierra-6 letter, verbatim, with beat numbers. Formatting shown as **bold**, <u>underline</u>, *italic*, and `> pull-quote`.

**[1]** *A Must-Read Message for Veterans Tired of Chasing Leads and Losing Jobs to the "Other Guys"*

**[2]** Dear Fellow Veteran,

**[3]** Let me know if this sounds like you:
- Tired of being "ghosted" by leads?
- Fed up paying $100 for a lead that curses you out when you call?
- Paid an agency $1,000s with no jobs to show for it?
- Worried where your next referral is going to come from?
- Watching sub-par contractors land *your* jobs?

**[4]** If so, you're not alone.

**Thousands of Veterans struggle with the same issues every day.**

**[5]** <u>You want to wake up to booked estimates.</u> You want to provide an excellent service. You want your time back doing what you love.

**You want more jobs without chasing down "no-shows" and "tire-kickers."**

You want to spend more time with your wife. Hell, you just want some spare time to fish, throw darts, or shoot some pool.

It doesn't feel like too much to ask for… after all, <u>you're good at what you do.</u>

**[6]** But each day, you're calling a lead that fills out your form, and then it **rings out to their voicemail.**

Or that HomeAdvisor (or Angi) lead is *"high-intent,"* but by the time you get them on the phone it's:

> "You're the 5th call today! Stop calling me, drop my number!"

You missed a call while working hard on the job site. By the time you get to it, **only a few hours later… "ghost."**

*You feel more like a professional voicemail-leaver than a business owner.*

When you finally get home, some kid from **ABC Agency calls you and asks if you'd like to sit in on a 60-minute "demo."** He wants your credit card so he can make a commission.

**[7]** Wake up. Rinse. Repeat. It's like Groundhog Day, ain't it?

**[8]** **It's aggravating as fire. I get it.**

**[9]** I'm not an agency, and I don't sell leads.

**[10]** My name is Ryan McKinney, and since boot camp everyone's called me RyMac. Half my platoon at Parris Island, SC was on the buddy program from Boston. It was a fun time, for sure. But…

**[11]** **I've worked with hundreds of home-service companies** over the last 15 years. My very first "client" was Tom, **a tree-removal guy.**

He came to me desperate with his last $1,500, and he bet it all on himself by giving it to me. 90 days later, Tom landed a huge contract to take care of a stretch of highway.

He called me with a vigor and excitement I'll never forget:

> "I LANDED A $50,000 CONTRACT — THAT WAS YOUR LEAD FROM WHATEVER YOU DID!" *(brand-red, all caps)*

It felt damn good to make a difference for Tom. He ended up growing into multiple locations, trucks, the whole nine.

**[12]** I decided this is what I wanted to do. <u>I wanted to help people.</u> It felt damn good. And since that day in 2011…

**[13]** **I've helped tens of thousands of business owners convert traffic into leads, and leads into sales.**

Removing the pain of doing day-to-day low-value tasks, so instead they're able to focus on high-value ones. Like providing excellent service, closing more deals, or taking their family on a vacation.

It led to Entrepreneur on Fire tracking me down to highlight and interview me. I had no idea I'd done anything "special." I figured this is what everyone did.

**[14]** Now it's 2026, and my focus is on **Veterans, in the home-service industry.** Veterans like you.

**[15]** My system is a culmination of millions in driven revenue. It's data-driven, built around **"The 3 Laws to <u>Landed</u> Jobs."**

My AI-powered sales system will:

**[16]**
- Reach out to every lead, in your voice and value, in under 5 minutes
- Chase down "no-shows" and get them back on the schedule
- Reach out and ask for reviews and/or referrals
- Text back missed calls in seconds, before they move on to the "other guys"
- Answer calls after hours, so that you wake up to booked sales calls
- Bring all communication channels, your CRM, pipeline manager, and reporting into a single login

**[17]** And if you don't have a website, or your current website sucks, **I'll build it FREE.**

Your website *is* an important piece to this puzzle. We'll make sure it's set up for success. There's no "half-stepping" here.

**[18]** **Most people see results the day it's deployed — as fast as 48 hours.**

**[19]** **Imagine never chasing down another lead again** or getting cursed out by a shared lead. Or feeling the gut-wrench of being "ghosted."

<u>Imagine never hearing another lead's voicemail.</u>

**Imagine regaining hours of your day back to do what you want to.**

**[20]** Let me give you the gift of time, sanity, and your dignity back, <u>for less than what your family dinner at Applebee's costs.</u>

**[21]** Let's have a 15-minute chat on the phone and see if it makes sense.

**[22]** Always got your back,
RyMac

## Beat-level notes

- **Beat 3 (the bullets)** — every bullet is a QUESTION the reader answers "yes… that's me" to. Each names a different pain vector: money wasted, being ignored, agency betrayal, referral anxiety, injustice (worse competitors winning). The injustice bullet lands last; it stings most. Use the reader's own vocabulary in scare quotes.
- **Beat 5 (empathy run)** — descend from business wants to human wants. The last wants are always personal (spouse, hobbies, time). Then the dignity line: "It doesn't feel like too much to ask for… after all, you're good at what you do." That line does the heavy lifting: it absolves them.
- **Beat 6 (agitation scenes)** — scenes, not summaries. A summary says "leads don't answer." A scene says "it rings out to their voicemail." Walk one full bad day chronologically: morning calls → the shared-lead insult → the missed call on site → the identity wound → the evening agency call. One scene must end in the enemy's spoken voice (pull-quote).
- **Beat 8 (the pivot)** — the most important sentence in the letter. It must be agreement, empathy, and nothing else. No pitch. Six words or fewer of pain agreement + "I get it."
- **Beat 11 (origin story)** — the client risked something real ("his last $1,500… he bet it all on himself"). The result has a timeframe and a dollar figure. The quote is the client's voice, not yours. The aftermath is casual ("the whole nine") — you don't need to sell your own proof.
- **Beat 16 (system bullets)** — map each bullet back to a beat-3/beat-6 pain. Outcome language only. The reader should feel the letter closing loops it opened.
- **Beat 18 (speed promise)** — this is the rymac-headline-copywriting formula restated: dream outcome + timeframe (+ without-pain implied). Keep it falsifiable.
- **Beat 19 (imagine run)** — one "Imagine" per agitation scene. Never introduce a NEW dream here; only negate pains already paid for.
- **Beat 23 (P.S.)** — never a summary. One sideways metaphor + the link. The P.S. is the second-most-read element of any letter; it earns its own tiny close.
