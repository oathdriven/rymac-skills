# Headlines for Paid-Traffic Landing Pages

The headline is 80% of the page's conversion power (Ogilvy: 5x as many people read the headline as anything else). Write it last-to-first: diagnose awareness → pick formula → write 20 versions → pick the survivor.

## Awareness levels

The single biggest headline decision is which awareness level the ad traffic arrives at (Schwartz, *Breakthrough Advertising*):

| Level | Traffic that lands here | Headline strategy |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Unaware | Cold broad Meta/YouTube | Identification/story/pattern-interrupt; never name product or problem directly. Rare — avoid sending unaware traffic to a squeeze page. |
| 2. Problem aware | Most cold Facebook/YouTube ads | Lead with the PAIN, named vividly in their words, then promise relief ("Tired of [problem]?") |
| 3. Solution aware | Google non-brand search | Lead with the RESULT + a new/better mechanism ("Get [outcome] with [mechanism]") |
| 4. Product aware | Retargeting | Differentiation, proof, "X without Y", guarantee, comparison |
| 5. Most aware | Brand search, cart abandoners | Product + offer + urgency ("Get 15% off your first order") |

Rule of thumb: **cold social = problem/solution-aware headlines · search = solution-aware · retargeting = product/most-aware.** Different traffic sources need different headlines on the same offer.

## The rules

1. **Message match beats everything.** The H1 repeats the promise of the ad that was clicked — same words, same offer. #1 rule for paid traffic (documented +66% lift).
2. **Clarity beats cleverness.** Sumo's 150k-headline dataset: straightforward beat "creative" 88% of the time (15.42% vs 7.1% conversion in the flagship test).
3. **Pass the 5-second Grunt Test** (StoryBrand): what is it, how does it make my life better, how do I get it.
4. **Specific and falsifiable.** If a competitor could have written it, cut it. "Recover 21% of abandoned carts" > "Recover more carts."
5. **Visualizable.** Concrete nouns over abstract adjectives — if they can't picture it, they won't feel it (Harry Dry).
6. **Lead with what they GET, not what it IS.** Outcome headlines beat category headlines (CXL 500-homepage analysis).
7. **Use numbers.** ~36% higher CTR; odd numbers edge even.
8. **6–12 words** (up to ~18 only for unfamiliar concepts).
9. **Stack Hormozi's value equation:** dream outcome ↑ + likelihood/proof ↑ + time ↓ + effort ↓. The ideal headline hits all four levers.
10. **Kill the #1 objection in the headline or subhead** — "without [dreaded thing]" is the highest-leverage clause in paid-traffic headlines.
11. **Enter the conversation already in their head** (Schwartz/Halbert): use the exact phrases mined from reviews/comments (see `niche-research.md`).
12. **Curiosity only works yoked to self-interest** — a named mechanism ("The 15-Minute X") beats pure cleverness.
13. **Write 20+ versions, ship the survivor.** Winners show up late. Headline tests swing conversion 27–104%.

## Headline formulas

Pick by awareness level. Write 3–5 candidates from each of the 3 best-fitting formulas, then cut.

| # | Formula | When | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Dream outcome] in [timeframe] without [pain/objection] | Solution-aware cold social — THE workhorse | "Get 50 Qualified Leads Per Week Without Cold Outreach" |
| 2 | How to [outcome] without [dreaded thing] | Problem/solution-aware; lead magnets | "How to Build a High-Converting Page Without Hiring a Developer" |
| 3 | Tired of [specific problem]? | Problem-aware cold traffic | "Delayed flight?" (ClaimCompass) |
| 4 | [Hard thing] in [short time] — or [risk reversal] | Product-aware offer pages | "Full body scan in 15 minutes — or your money back" |
| 5 | [Avatar]: [outcome] with [mechanism] that [kills effort] | Niche-targeted paid social | "Coffee shop owners: increase sales 20% with an ordering system that runs itself" |
| 6 | Hormozi MAGIC: [reason why] + [avatar] + [goal] + [interval] + [container] | Challenge/webinar funnels | "Free 6-Week Summer-Ready Challenge for Busy Dads in Dallas" |
| 7 | How [someone like you] got [result] in [timeframe] | Retargeting, B2B, case-study pages | "How Shopify Lifted Conversions 35% in 30 Days" |
| 8 | The only [category] that [differentiator] | Product-aware, crowded market, search | "The only CRM your reps will actually use" |
| 9 | Say goodbye to [pain]. Say hello to [benefit]. | Problem-aware before/after | "Say goodbye to spreadsheet chaos. Say hello to one-click reports." |
| 10 | [Number] [avatar]s use [product] to [outcome] | Trust-bottleneck retargeting | "47,000 agencies run their reporting on AgencyAnalytics" |
| 11 | Stop [wrong thing]. [Right thing] instead. | Competitor-conquest, solution-aware | "Stop renting your stack. Own it." |
| 12 | Get [specific offer] on [product] | Most-aware retargeting/brand search | "Get 15% Off Your First Order" |
| 13 | If you can [low-bar skill], you can [impressive outcome] | Effort-objection audiences, DIY/courses | "If you can send an email, you can run this entire CRM" |
| 14 | The [named mechanism] that [outcome] | Sophisticated/fatigued markets (see niche-research.md stages 3–4) | "The 15-Minute Ad-Scent Audit That Cuts Your CPL in Half" |
| 15 | [Outcome], guaranteed — or [consequence to us] | High-ticket, risk is last objection | "Page one in 90 days — or we work free until you are" |
| 16 | [Exact ad headline, repeated verbatim] | ALWAYS the first candidate for a dedicated paid-traffic page | Ad "Fully Free for Life" → H1 "Fully Free for Life" (+66%) |

## Subheadline

Job: make the headline's promise **believable and concrete**. Emotional headline → rational subhead. Carries the mechanism, the proof, the specificity, and softens the #1 objection. 10–30 words (≤2x headline length). A documented headline+subhead rewrite from "we" language to "you get" language lifted conversion 67.8%.

Formulas:
1. **Mechanism:** "[Product] is a [category] that [how it delivers the promise]"
2. **Proof stack:** "Join [number] [avatar]s who [outcome] — [rating]★ on [platform]"
3. **Outcome expansion:** "Get [benefit], [benefit], and [benefit] — without [objection]"
4. **Objection kill:** "No [feared thing]. No [feared thing]. Cancel anytime."
5. **How-it-works:** "Just [step], [step], and [result]"
6. **Who it's for:** "Built for [specific avatar] who [situation]"
7. **Guarantee:** "[Bold promise] — or [what we do about it]"
8. **Ad continuation:** restate the ad's concrete offer + terms when the H1 mirrors the ad hook

## Message match

1. H1 uses identical or near-identical wording to the ad headline clicked. One page (or dynamic headline) per ad angle — never one generic page for all ads.
2. Match all four layers: claim, language, visual (hero echoes the ad creative), audience framing.
3. The offer promised in the ad is the primary above-the-fold element — never make them hunt ("ad scent": break the scent and they bounce in seconds).
4. Carry the match through CTA copy and the thank-you page.
5. Google Ads: Dynamic Text Replacement per keyword. Meta: one variant per ad angle (pain-led ad → pain-led H1).
6. On Google, message match also raises Quality Score → cheaper clicks; the lift compounds with spend.

## Data points

- Message match into the H1: **+66% conversion** (KlientBoost); an insurance LP hit **13.47% conversion (~3x category average)** primarily via headline match.
- Plain beats clever **88%** of the time (Sumo, 150k headlines).
- "You get" rewrite: **+67.8%** (98.7% confidence).
- Attention ratio to 1:1: **+31% and +40%** lifts (Unbounce).
- Headline optimization range: **27–104%** conversion improvement.
- Benchmarks: ~5% is a strong LP conversion rate; top pages hit 20–25%+.
