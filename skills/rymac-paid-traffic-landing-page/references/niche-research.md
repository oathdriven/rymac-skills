# Niche Pain Research — the 20-Minute Sprint

Copywriting is ~80% research. The highest-converting headlines are lightly-edited customer quotes ("steal it directly from the mouths of your prospects" — Joanna Wiebe; Copyhackers' mined-quote rehab headline drove +400% clicks). If the copy is hard to write, the research isn't done.

## Intake questions

Answer all 12 before writing copy (ask the user for what they know; mine the rest):

1. Who exactly is the buyer — one person, not a demographic? What does a bad Tuesday look like for them?
2. What is the ONE costliest, most emotionally-charged pain (Halbert's "dominant resident emotion")?
3. What have they already tried, and how did it fail them? (This is where skepticism comes from.)
4. What/who do they blame? (Agree with their blame narrative; never blame the reader.)
5. What do they secretly desire most — identity-level ("fire my boss"), not feature-level?
6. What are they afraid of / angry about?
7. Top 3 daily frustrations? (Best source of hyper-specific hooks.)
8. What language do THEY use — verbatim phrases, what they'd type into Google at 2am?
9. Awareness stage of the ad traffic (see `headlines.md`)?
10. Sophistication stage of the market, 1–5 (below)?
11. JTBD forces: what PUSHES them off the status quo, PULLS them to a new solution, what ANXIETY blocks switching, what HABIT keeps them stuck? Push + pull must outweigh anxiety + habit.
12. What claim will they call BS on? (Dictates the proof element.)

## The sprint

Run these steps with WebSearch. Stop mining when quotes start repeating (saturation = signal; 30–50 quotes is plenty).

- [ ] **1. Define targets (2 min):** who's the buyer, what solution do they seek, what do they use NOW, where is that reviewed. Output: 3 competitor/alternative names + 3 communities + 1–2 book topics.
- [ ] **2. Sophistication scan (3 min):** search competitors' headlines / Meta Ad Library for the niche. Output: stage 1–5 diagnosis + a worn-out-claims list (never repeat these).
- [ ] **3. Mine 3 sources in parallel (8 min):**
  - `site:amazon.com inurl:"product-reviews" "tired of" [niche]` (variants: "frustrated by", "wish I had", "finally")
  - `site:reddit.com [niche] "does anyone else" OR "so frustrated" OR "what finally worked"` — read top comments, not just posts
  - Software niche → G2/Capterra "dislikes" section; local-service niche → Google 1–3★ reviews of COMPETITORS
  - Capture VERBATIM quotes into a 3-column sheet: quote / category (pain, desire, objection, failed-alternative, trigger) / source
- [ ] **4. Tally + rank (4 min):** frequency × emotional charge = hierarchy. Pick: 1 dominant pain, 1 dream outcome, top 3 objections, top 2 failed alternatives, the trigger moment. Discard the rest — one page, one pain.
- [ ] **5. Awareness call (3 min):** what does this specific ad traffic know when it lands?
- [ ] **6. Fill the mapping table (5 min)** — see below.
- [ ] **7. Sanity gates (2 min):** headline claim one stage ahead of the market? Every key phrase something a prospect actually said? One proof element per objection? If any gate fails, mine 5 more minutes.

## Mining sources

Ranked by signal-per-minute. Mine the middle stars: 2–3★ = unmet expectations/objections, 5★ = desire language, 1★ = category-level fears your risk reversal must answer.

1. **Amazon book reviews** (books on the problem, not products) — self-description of the pain, "I used to… now I…" transformation language, failed advice.
2. **Reddit** — raw pain, blame narratives, insider vocabulary; upvotes = shared pain; pain repeating across subs = market-wide.
3. **G2/Capterra** (software) — switch triggers ("we moved from X because…"), "dislikes" = objections.
4. **Google/Yelp competitor reviews** (local) — 1–3★ = the fears to kill (no-shows, surprise pricing, unreturned calls); praise = table stakes to promise.
5. **YouTube comments** on niche how-to/rant videos — "this is me exactly" confessionals.
6. **Facebook groups** — repeated questions = unsolved pains.
7. **Meta Ad Library** — current sophistication level; what claims are worn out.

## Sophistication stages

(Schwartz. A property of the MARKET — diagnose from competitors' ads. Write one stage AHEAD of the loudest incumbent; repeating the worn-out claim is invisible, skipping two stages is confusing.)

| Stage | Market state | Headline must |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Virgin — first claim in | Simple naked claim of the desire ("Lose 30 lbs") |
| 2 | Claim copied by competitors | Same claim, ENLARGED to the believable limit ("…in 30 days without hunger") |
| 3 | Heard every claim | Lead with the MECHANISM; claim drops to subhead ("New [mechanism] does [old promise]") — most competitive niches sit here |
| 4 | Mechanisms commoditized | Enlarged/improved mechanism ("Not another chatbot — an AI employee that answers, books, and follows up") |
| 5 | Burned-out, cynical | Identification — speak to who they are/want to become; identity, story, or radical honesty leads |

## Pain → copy mapping

| Research finding | Page element it feeds |
|---|---|
| #1 dominant pain (frequency × charge) | **Headline** — often the verbatim quote itself |
| Trigger event / JTBD push | **Eyebrow / first line** — name the moment they're in |
| Failed alternatives | **Mechanism/differentiation** in the subhead ("It's not your fault — it's…") |
| Blame narrative | **Problem framing** — agree with it |
| Dream outcome (verbatim after-state) | **Subheadline + CTA copy** — CTA finishes "I want to ___" in their words |
| Objections / anxieties | **Risk reversal** under the CTA |
| Skepticism about the core claim | **Trust element** — proof that mirrors the exact doubt |
| Habit force ("switching = work") | Effort-killer clause ("live in 15 minutes", "takes 30 seconds") |
| Insider vocabulary | **Every element** — if they say "no-shows", never write "appointment attrition" |

## Heuristics

- Verbatim beats paraphrase — paraphrasing launders out the voice you came for.
- One dominant pain per page. A page about three pains converts on none.
- Join the conversation already in their head; ride the existing emotion, don't install a new one.
- Never blame the reader — externalize the failure to the tools/advice they were given.
- Every claim needs a matched proof element; "I doubted X too, but…" testimonials beat generic praise.
- CTA = their desire in their words, first person ("Get my Sundays back"), never "Submit".
