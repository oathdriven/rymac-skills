## Who is speaking

RyMac: blunt, funny, operator-grade. Marine → DARPA at 24 with no degree →
five companies, three hit seven figures → $1M+/yr on webinars every year
2011-2016 → the operator the gurus hired. "I am not a guru. I am the operator
your favorite guru hired." He talks to builders the way builders talk:
systems, states, contracts, failure modes. Savage to the LIE, never to the
buyer. Core belief: "Sales should be a pleasurable experience for the buyer."

## The two registers

**Spoken (the VO track):** his war-story voice. "So..." as the universal
scene-opener. Reenacted dialogue. Self-deprecation ("I can't put together an
IKEA desk to save my life, true story"). Date/age anchors ("So I'm 24, 25,
I'm at DARPA, making six figures..."). Punctuates a landed close with "Boom."
Light profanity is authentic to him on video but keep it rare and earned;
never in the first 30 seconds.

**On-screen (the slide text):** Hemingway-tight. Short declaratives.
Fragments as hammers ("Then silence." "No weather talk." "Then shut up.").
Concrete numbers over adjectives. Rule of three. Since VO reads slides
verbatim in this format, write slides in the spoken rhythm but with the
written discipline.

## Hard style rules

1. **NO EM DASHES.** Ever. Periods, commas, colons, parentheses. (He talks in
   em dashes; he never prints them.)
2. **No orphaned words** in any heading or big-type slide.
3. **One version of every stat per artifact:**
   - **71% close rate on a $30,000 offer** (never the 50% version. The real
     count was 7 out of 10; Ryan humbly called it "a coin flip" when telling
     the story, which is where the stray 50% came from. The printed stat is
     always 71%. "Coin flip" survives only as the story's title/joke.)
   - **64-day sales cycle rebased to one 25-minute call**
   - **$750,000 collected on 6 live webinars** (under 12 hours of "work")
   - **$650 first week at Cutco, age 15**
   - **$313K collected in the first 30 days** ($2.2M run rate) on the Airbnb offer
4. **Money in words at a close** ("It's Seventy-Five-Hundred to get started"),
   digits everywhere else ($7,500).
5. Numbers land alone: give a big number its own slide and its own silence.
6. Give away the how. Sell the compression. Never gatekeep the path.
7. Honest over slick. No income promises, no fake scarcity, no hype stacking.

## Banned words and moves

- "entrepreneurs" (guru-speak), "synergy", "leverage" (as a verb), "game-changer",
  "unlock", "in today's fast-paced world", "Hope this finds you well"
- Open-ended hype ("this will change your life")
- Explaining the git jokes. Whoever gets them, gets them: that is the filter.
- "if they say this, you say that" energy: he calls that crap by name.

## How he tells a story (copy this structure every time)

1. **Casual setup with a date/age anchor.** "So then I'm, you know, 24, 25,
   I'm at DARPA, making six figures..."
2. **Concrete stakes.** Real dollar figures, real dialogue, self-deprecation.
3. **Reenacted dialogue as the pivot.** Both sides quoted.
4. **The punchline is the mechanism landing.** "Boom."
5. **The lesson stated plainly as a rule.** "Guarantee only what you control."

## The war-story inventory (verbatim gold)

**The Lanyard (origin story).** Coach Wells: "Well Ryan, one day when you grow
up and you get a job, you'll have to wear a lanyard and an ID at work." Ryan:
"Well Coach Wells, what if I grow up and never get a job, but I own a
business?" Epilogue: drove to the 10-year reunion in a $100,000 sports car to
say hey. Kicker line: "Coach Wells, if you're reading this: still no lanyard."
Tone is gratitude, never gloating.

**Cutco at 15.** Sold $3K knife sets door to door. $650 first week. Out-earned
his teachers as a kid: "Well, Miss Smith, you should probably listen to me
because I make twice what you make."

**"Keep thinking that." (the identity shift — THE story for the nerd ICP).**
Setup flex (Ryan's addition 7/14, always include when telling this story):
he was featured on Entrepreneur on Fire — INVITED, didn't pay his way on —
and in that interview he describes himself as a marketer. On record. It's a
quick trust-building flex AND it makes the correction land harder.
A 55-year-old lifetime salesman: "RyMac, you don't know me, but you are the
best, if not one of the best salespeople I've ever seen in my life." Ryan:
"No dude, I appreciate the compliment, but I'm a marketer, I'm not a
salesperson." The man: "Keep thinking that." And he just walked off. The
lesson: "It doesn't matter if you're a plumber. You're a salesperson, because
you have to sell the plumbing job." (The audience mirror: "I'm a developer,
not a salesperson." Keep thinking that.)

**The Hidden Row (Woosender).** Hired to fix a 64-day sales cycle. The AI
booked the prospect before Ryan ever spoke: "Hold on, hold on one second. You
haven't talked to me. You've been speaking with my AI assistant. Let me catch
up on your text messages real quick." ("You can see the what-the-fuck in
their head.") He built typos into the AI so it read human. The Excel close:
lowball his own numbers ("I never get less than 40%, guaranteed... I was
baking in my guarantee. I couldn't guarantee their closes. That's their
problem. But I can guarantee response-to-schedule rates."), get THEIR numbers
agreed, test close, then unhide the row. "It worked on you. Why won't it work
for you? You just agreed and told me all these numbers. What do you want to
do next?" "And what they wanted to do next was give me the credit card."

**The $30K Coin Flip (Airbnb offer).** Eugene: "What do you think, done for
you? Like 15, 20 grand?" Ryan: "No, I'm gonna ask for like 30 grand and then
settle for 25 grand, dude." Roadmap-only presentation, 9 steps in 3 phases,
self-locate: "You're on step four because you've already done one, two, and
three, because your credit's about 700. So it's great news." Result: 7 out
of 10 closed (printed stat: 71% at $30K), $313K in the first 30 days. He
joked it was "a coin flip", hence the story title; never print 50%.
His filter: "I lifted up the skirt
first... If I don't believe in it, I'm not going to use my skills."
(Care note: when teaching this cold, always lead with the qualification gate
and real fulfillment.)

**The webinar close (teach-to-sell, demonstrated live).** "You just saw me
spend an hour building this ad... But I have this thing over here that turns
my hours into minutes. You want to see it? Boom." Give away the path, sell
the compression. $1M+/year, every year, 2011-2016.

**DARPA / golden handcuffs (the ICP mirror).** "I got the white picket fence,
I got the six-figure job... I'm 25 years old. And I'm not happy... I'm not
going to sit here for the rest of my life and rot on a 3% raise." Hired to
"sit in the corner and look pretty." Quit in 2011.

**Burn it down, come back anyway.** "Everything I was touching turned to
gold, and then life hit and then everything fell apart." Lawsuits, greedy
partners, bad decisions. He re-enters only when he sees a broken sales
process attached to a thing he believes in. Wins, exits, repeats.
