# CTC Course Map — the videos, their beat maps, and product tie-ins

Every video: under 10 minutes, standalone-watchable, ends with a bridge to
the next one. The course gives away the whole path (teach-to-sell, the
webinar mechanic that made $1M+/yr). The compression it sells: closectl, the
Skool community, and Ryan.

## Product tie-in rules

- **One soft product touch per video, max.** Placed in the bridge, never
  mid-teach. Generous tone: "Free, because I would rather you be dangerous."
- Framework/objection videos bridge to **closectl** ("the framework as a
  console you keep open during the call") or **The Lurker** kit
  (committoclose.com, free).
- Lead-gen videos bridge to the **free Skool marketing school**.
- Never a hard pitch, never scarcity, never income promises. The receipts do
  the selling.

## Video 1 — The Truth About Sales: It's a State Machine, Not Scripts
Target 6-8 min. The high-level overview of the whole framework.

Beat map:
1. Hook: the wrong belief. They think they need scripts, charisma, a guru
   voice. ("I'm a developer, not a salesperson." Keep thinking that.)
2. Stakes: the receipts. 71% at $30K. 64 days to one call. No demo, no deck.
   The edge was never the tools.
3. Teach: scripts are "if they say this, you say that" and they collapse
   off-page. A state machine only needs to know its current state and the
   transition that earns the next one. Walk the 7 states at altitude (one
   slide each, stage 3 amber): lurk, rapport, diagnosis, COMMITMENT, present,
   test-close, close. "Every stage is insurance for a later stage." Skipping
   stage 3 = the merge conflict.
4. The doctrine slide: "Never tell them anything you can get them to say
   themselves."
5. Bridge: next video walks every stage with the exact lines. Soft touch:
   The Lurker kit, free.

## Video 2 — Stages 0-3: Earn the Commitment (Ryan's split, 7/14)
Target 8-9 min. The framework's first half, deliberately ending ON the
commitment because stage 3 is the most important stage and the brand itself.
A few minutes of feel per stage: what it does, why it exists, the verbatim
line, and the transition that earns the next stage.

Beat map:
1. Hook: "Seven states, indexed from zero. Arrays start at zero and so does
   selling."
2. Stage by stage (framework.md is canon): 0 lurk (weave, never recite) →
   1 rapport + the Business Doctor frame (ends in a yes) → 2 diagnosis (the
   doctor questions + magic wand) → 3 COMMITMENT ("would you be opposed",
   the spade in your pocket, the stage everyone skips, the name of the
   brand).
3. Land stage 3 as the cliffhanger AND the climax: no commit, no close.
   Everything before it existed to earn this one safe little "no".
4. Bridge: "You have their word in your pocket. Next video: how you cash it."
   Soft touch: closectl's stage-3 conflict gate ("the graph will tell on
   you").

## Video 3 — Stages 4-6: Present, Test, Merge
Target 8-9 min. The framework's back half: cash the commitment on the same
call.

Beat map:
1. Hook: recap in one breath. You lurked, you framed, you diagnosed, and they
   said they would not be opposed. Now do not blow it with a demo.
2. Stage 4 present (roadmap, self-locate, "nobody argues with their own pin",
   never demo the tech) → stage 5 test-close (truth surfaces while it costs
   nothing; isolate if price jumps the queue) → stage 6 close (price in
   words, "What do you want to do next?", then shut up).
3. The thread: each stage exists to make a later stage cheap. Objections at
   the close are just unpaid debts from skipped stages.
4. Bridge: "You don't need a script. You need to know where you are." That is
   literally what closectl is: the 7-stage rail on screen during your live
   call, your lines loaded. Next video: where the calls come from.

## Video 4 — Build a Lead Generation Machine
Target 8-9 min. Load leadgen.md. THE LAW opens it: "Once a lead is generated,
it is a sales problem. Marketing gets the lead. Sales gets the money."

Beat map:
1. Hook: the best framework in the world starves without at-bats. / "You
   built a whiz-bang and gave it one bullet?"
2. Stakes: the numbers (16% reply flagship, honest ladder 5-10% good).
3. Teach: the machine in 8 modules (burner domains → ratios → auth → warmup
   → the verified micro-list → plain-text copy that earns a "yes" → the
   threading sequence → speed to lead). Credit Lead Gen Jay proudly and
   disclose the affiliate relationship with humor (see leadgen.md): "Jay's
   system, my receipts, and yes, I'm an affiliate."
4. The handoff: a "yes" reply is a lead, and now it is a sales problem. The
   machine feeds the state machine.
5. Bridge: free Skool marketing school (the whole machine, taught free).

## Video 5 — The 3 Ways to Handle Any and All Objections
Target 8-9 min. The committoclose.com objections section brought to life.

Beat map:
1. Hook: "All objections are lies. It's always about the price."
2. Stakes: you do not need a thousand rebuttals. Three handlers cover
   everything. "You can't spade everything. You can't isolate everything.
   You can't 1-10 everything. But you can use those 3 for any and all types
   of objections."
3. Teach each handler with its reenacted dialogue (framework.md verbatim):
   - The Spade (leads with "I'm confused"; the wife walkthrough; the
     two-weeks truth; the spade origin story)
   - Isolation (flush the stack; the price version ends "what can you swing
     today?")
   - The Scale (the 1-10; they dictate their own closing conditions)
4. The precondition: every handler leans on the stage 3 commitment. No
   commitment, no spade. The system pre-kills objections; the handlers mop up.
5. Recap stack: every handler ends in a question. Then you close again.
6. Bridge: closectl's fumble log + Sparring Partner ("fumble the spade eight
   times at 6am, nobody watching").
