## The machine in one line

Burner domains → warmed mailboxes → scraped and verified ICP list → plain-text
sequence that earns a one-word "yes" → reply inside 5 minutes → 15-minute
call → the framework takes over.

## The teachable modules (course spine)

1. **Never send cold from your real domain.** Lookalike burner domains
   (getyourbrand.com, tryyourbrand.com) that redirect to the main site. A
   blacklist hit on a burner never touches the brand.
2. **The ratios.** 3 mailboxes per domain. 20-40 cold sends/day per mailbox
   (30 is the working number). Scale by adding domains, never by cranking
   volume. Default starter order: 30 mailboxes across 10 domains (~$225 first
   month, ~$105/mo after via Inbox Insiders; domains + SMTP + DNS auth +
   warmup all provision automatically).
3. **Authentication is mandatory.** SPF, DKIM, DMARC on every sending domain
   (Google/Yahoo enforce it; spam complaint rate above 0.3% is a cliff).
4. **Warmup: 4+ weeks minimum, warmup score 95+, before the first cold send.**
   Keep warmup running forever at 10-20% of volume. Build the list and copy
   while you wait.
5. **The list.** Scrape one vertical + one metro at a time (Consulti: 500M+
   B2B, Google Maps locals). AI-qualify every lead against the ICP. Verify
   every email (MillionVerifier); bounce over 3% is a hard stop. Wave 1 is
   the 80-100 cleanest fits: prove conversion before scaling. Sub-200
   micro-segmented lists hit 15-20% reply vs ~8% on 500+ blasts.
6. **The copy.** Plain text. No images, no links in email 1, no tracking
   pixel. Optimize for replies, not opens. Talk WITH the lead, not AT them.
   Personalization = relevance, not flattery. Soft question CTA every time
   (interest CTA ~12% reply vs meeting-ask ~7%). Follow-ups are new angles,
   never "just checking in" (42% of replies come from follow-ups).
   Banned: "Hope this finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", synergy-speak,
   em dashes (the AI tell).
7. **The sequence shape.** 5-6 emails. Blank subjects from email 2 on so they
   thread. Awareness-backward: E1 hits the most-aware pain, the last email is
   the reframe (the heaviest artillery). The whole sequence exists to earn a
   one-word "yes" reply; a reply-triggered sub-sequence delivers the value
   asset, then the booking link. Recycle non-responders with a fresh angle
   4-8 weeks later: free at-bats.
8. **Speed to lead.** A human reply within 5 minutes of theirs. Then the
   15-minute call, and it is a sales problem now: the state machine takes it.

## Verbatim copy exemplar (Ryan's "betting man" opener, Sierra-6)

"Hey {{firstName}}, one vet to another. I'll be blunt with you and make a
couple of bets: I'll bet you're not happy with how many of your leads turn
into ghosts instead of an estimate. I'll bet you're good at landing the job,
you'd just like more at-bats. I'll bet you don't know the real reason they
ghost, and figure you've got a lead problem. I have a 60-second breakdown
that explains the 3 laws to landing more jobs. I'll bet you're breaking at
least 2 of them. Wanna' bet? Reply 'yes' and I'll send the breakdown."

And the reframe (last-email artillery): "When a lead goes cold it feels like
a junk lead. Usually it is not. The homeowner called, nobody got to them fast
enough, and they went with whoever did. That is a speed thing, not a lead
thing."
