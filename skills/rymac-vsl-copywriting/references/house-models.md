## 1. Sierra-6 fold video — the pacing model

Live as `homepagevideo.mp4` on sierra6ai.com. Source: `sierra6-funnel-source/gen-slides.js` (17-slide) and `build_slides.py` (19-slide, adds two price-anchor slides). ~320 spoken words → ~2:15–2:30 → ≈5–7s/slide.

**Format spec (what the build actually used):** 16:9 white background, all text centered. Arial Bold. Base #111111; red #C8102E highlight on 1–3 emotional/numeric runs per slide; green #16A34A reserved for the final signoff slide only. Font size inversely scales with word count: 42pt on the 5-word CTA slide down to 26–27pt on ~28-word slides. Production notes ride as gray italic footer text on the slide.

**19-slide beat map:**
1. Hook question (why leads ghost you)
2. Gut-punch reframe — "it's not your prices, and it's not your work — **it's a speed and follow-up problem.**"
3. The 5-minute rule ("No 'about.' This is science.")
4. Stakes (100× / guaranteed ghosting)
5. Identity — "I'm RyMac — a Marine, two dogs, and a laptop. And I'm **not an agency**."
6. Credibility (20 years + the 3 Laws of Landed Jobs)
7–9. One Law per slide — Law name in red on its own line, plain-English payoff line under it
10. Offer — done for you: site, follow-up, calendar; "invisible automation that feels human, live in as little as 48 hours"
11. Anchor 1 (agency $2,000+/mo)
12. Anchor 2 (his old $7,500 + $2,500/mo)
13. Proof + price tease (3–10×, "$100 is going to feel a little crazy")
14. Pain-recap ellipsis stack ("...staring at a database of 'leads' you already paid for...")
15. CTA — 5 words, biggest type: "...book a **free 15-minute call.**"
16. Risk reversal (not pushy, no contracts, no setup fees)
17. Takeaway ("not a fit, I'll tell you — part ways as friends")
18. Real scarcity (first 10 veterans, $100/mo, price going up)
19. Final CTA ("click the green button below") + green signoff "Talk soon — always got your back. / RyMac"

**Signature moves to reuse:** the setup-line/punch-line two-paragraph slide with the punch highlighted · the CTA split across two slides for a hard pause (pain-recap ellipsis slide → 5-word CTA slide at max type) · Law/pillar name in brand color on its own line · credibility fused with identity in one slide · the takeaway slide directly after risk reversal.

## 2. The 5:1 compression pattern

The slide script is the full spoken transcript (~1,500 words ≈ 7 min) distilled ~5:1 into ~320 words. The rules that did it:
- **Same beat order** — compression never reorders the argument.
- **Cut all stories, studies, and asides** — each beat keeps only its single strongest sentence. (The HBR/Oldroyd study became "This is science." — four words carrying the entire citation.)
- **Numbers survive; context dies.** Anchors, multipliers, and prices stay; their backstories go.
- ⚠️ **Compression drift check:** numbers changed between transcript and slides ("at least six follow-ups" → "12 to 24 times"; "most quit after the second" → "most guys quit after one"). When compressing, re-verify every number against the source claim — drift is a guardrail risk.

Use this pattern any time a long VSL/webinar/transcript becomes a fold video: transcript → beat list → strongest sentence per beat → chunk to slides.

## 4. Sierra-6 / RyMac de facto voice

Until `voice-sierra6.md` / `voice-rymac.md` exist, these are the sources of truth: the fold-video slide script, `sierra6-funnel-source/OathDriven-Video-VSL-Transcript.txt`, `sierra6-funnel-source/OathDriven-Copy-Bank.md`, and the cold-email-outreach skill positioning.

- **Sierra-6 ICP:** veteran-owned home-service/construction, owner-operator, ≤10 employees, commercial and residential both. Excluded: franchises, chains, >10 employees, non-veteran.
- **Problem sold:** speed-to-lead + follow-up ("it's not your prices, it's not your work"). Promise vocabulary: booked estimates, filled calendar, stop getting ghosted, double lead-to-estimate.
- **The 3 Laws (of Landed Jobs):** 1. Speed — every lead answered <5 min, day or night (Oldroyd/HBR: 5–10 min delay = 400% drop, 30 min = gone). 2. Persistence — most jobs close after the 5th touch; most quit after the 2nd. 3. Right Message at the Right Time — Schwartz awareness journey; "we talk *with* your leads, not at them — so they book instead of bolt."
- **Offer:** DFY — CRM + dashboard + free website + pipeline + automations, "the last login you'll ever need," live in as little as 48 hours. Founder offer: first 10 veterans, flat $100/mo, no contracts, no setup fees. Anchors: agency $2k+/mo; his old $7,500 + $2,500/mo.
- **Copy-Bank bullet formula:** `[Pain]? The only [mechanism] that [how] to deliver [benefit] within [timeframe] / so you can [pain removal]` — 15 pre-built Pain→Mechanism→Benefit bullets parked for below-fold use.
- **RyMac persona:** "a Marine, two dogs, and a laptop — and I'm not an agency." Kanorado, KS; VFW Post 1142 origin; signoff "Talk soon — always got your back." Role: public teaching brand, top-of-funnel; the 9 cheatsheets are the YouTube spine.

## 5. Guardrails master list

The voice doc at write time overrules this list — but audit every script against it:

1. **Allodra ownership claim (hard rule):** publicly claim only "buy it once, own the license, no monthly rent, no growth tax." No "deploy to your own cloud / nobody can lock you out" until reseller rights are live. (Status has been in flux — check `voice-allodra.md` at write time; exception: DFY delivery that literally deploys into client accounts.)
2. **No money-back guarantee for Allodra, ever** — delivered code can't be clawed back. Replace with: one-time · own forever · lifetime updates · step-by-step onboarding. No 1:1 onboarding calls promised.
3. **"Guarantee" is a literal claim** — back it or soften it until the earnings disclaimer is wired. **3–10× returns** claims need that disclaimer.
4. **"Featured on Entrepreneur on Fire" — verify before it appears in any public asset.**
5. **$3,855.66 avg customer spend** stays OUT of the spoken anchor (clutters the $7,500/$2,500 story) — social-proof sections only. **$2.36M run-rate case:** present as "the system," never name the niche.
6. **Never sell leads** (Sierra-6 hard rule) — no lead-gen promise language, ever.
7. **Villain = tool/category/incumbent/practice — never the viewer's own failure.**
8. **Real scarcity only** — "first 10 veterans" / builds-per-month are real caps; no timers, no invented cohorts. Founder members may not share founder pricing (a condition of the deal).
9. **GBP review-reply/Q&A channel is NOT built** — pull it or mark "coming soon."
10. **Nurture recipe (12–24 touch) is hands-on per client**, not turnkey — honest only because Ryan configures it.
11. **Dead numbers:** no "888 line" — the live number is +1 346 660 2260.
12. **Entity hygiene:** OathDriven LLC stays out of SaaS marketing copy.
13. **Compression drift** (see §2): re-verify every number after compressing a longer script.
